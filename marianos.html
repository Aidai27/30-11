Mariano’s Pickup Mobile Enhancements

When you're focused on the user experience, you can't help but choose, assess, and think about ways to improve the products and services you frequently use. As a frequent Kroger customer who uses their pickup service, I found several ways to improve the overall customer experience. Knowing that the COVID-19 epidemic has driven many people to convert to online grocery shopping, I thought it would be interesting to look at ways to enhance the Mariano’s Pickup experience.

The Challange

COVID-19 is impacting how many Individuals shopping today. Customers are increasingly dependent on grocery shopping, particularly online grocery shopping, due to a lack of restaurant and eating out choices. Many supermarkets provide online shopping and curbside grocery pick-up, while others allow third-party services such as Instacart to shop for clients. In 2016, Kroger launched Clicklist, currently known as Pickup, an online ordering service that allows consumers to purchase online for their products and pick them up at the store of their choice .

With the increase in demand and huge shift for the industry as a consequence of COVID-19, Kroger needs to improve their online shopping and pickup/delivery experience to meet the diverse tastes of their consumers, who are depending on their Pickup service more than ever. I decided to investigate how Kroger might improve their checkout and post-checkout experience.

User Stories

As a Pickup customer, I want to view suggestions of and select substitutes for my item in my cart pre-checkout, so that I do not have to worry about my shopper selecting an unsatisfactory substitute.

As Pickup customer, I need the ability to chat directly with my shopper while they are shopping for my groceries, so that I can answer questions or approve substitutions.

As Pickup customer, I want the ability to select plastic, paper, or personal bags for my groceries to be bagged in during checkout, so that I can make environmentally friendly choices if I prefer.

Virtual moderated testing of the concepts was planned and carried out utilizing video conference, screen sharing, and Figma prototyping.

Result

The checkout experience was more clear to users, and the ability to choose alternatives enhanced their trust that their shopper would select suitable goods.


Two out of five interviewees expressed a need for a better option for applying

paper coupons to orders.


As a Pickup client, one out of every three people interviewed stated that the opportunity to select bagging choice was a modest but crucial feature.
